Who I am

About Me

I'm a Mechanical Engineering student at Stanford, originally from Monterrey, Mexico. I came here drawn to engineering — and stayed for the realization that what I love most isn't just understanding how things work, but actually building them and discovering where your sketch was wrong in the best possible way.

At the Hollberg Lab, I design and fabricate custom hardware for a precision methane laser spectroscopy system — building 3D-printed adapters to motorize manual stages, iterating on windshield wiper mechanisms to protect optical components, and learning what it really means to prepare a lab instrument for the field. It's where tolerance analysis stops being a homework problem and starts mattering.

Outside the lab, I've led logistics for Stanford's first Designathon, directed large-scale construction projects with Techo, and served as community engagement lead for Stanford Women in Design — roles that taught me that good engineering isn't just about the object, it's about the people it's made for. I bring that same attention to detail and care for the user into everything I build.

My projects live at the intersection of engineering rigor and aesthetic intention — things that are well-made and feel that way.
